## **Introduction**

Higher education plays a vital role in the social, economic, and technological development of a country. To improve the quality, access, and governance of higher education in India, the Government of India launched the **Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA)** in **2013**.

RUSA is a comprehensive centrally sponsored scheme aimed at reforming and developing the higher education system in India.

---

## **Meaning and Definition**

RUSA is defined as:

‚ÄúA comprehensive scheme for reforming and developing higher education in India.‚Äù

It was launched by the **Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD)** as a major initiative to transform State universities and colleges.

---

## **Background and Progression**

RUSA is part of a continuous educational development framework:

* **1997 ‚Äì SSA** was launched for universal elementary education
* **2009 ‚Äì RMSA** was introduced for secondary education
* **2013 ‚Äì RUSA** was launched as the next step for higher education

Thus, RUSA is the natural progression in strengthening education from primary to university level.

---

## **Purpose of RUSA**

The main purpose of RUSA is to create a new central organization that will:

* Introduce reforms in higher education
* Assist in planning developmental schemes
* Supervise State-administered universities and colleges

RUSA aims to improve the overall higher education landscape by providing support, funding, and autonomy to institutions.

---

## **Key Functions of RUSA**

RUSA performs several important functions to enhance higher education quality and accessibility.

---

### **1. Upgrading Universities to International Standards**

RUSA focuses on upgrading selected universities so that they can achieve **international standards** in teaching, research, and infrastructure.

---

### **2. Establishment of New Universities**

RUSA permits the opening of new universities to expand access to higher education and meet the increasing demand among youth.

---

### **3. Cluster College System**

Under RUSA, colleges located near a university are linked together to form **cluster colleges**. This promotes:

* Collaboration
* Resource sharing
* Better academic support

---

### **4. Development of Model Colleges**

RUSA supports the upgrading of full-fledged degree colleges into **model colleges**. These institutions serve as benchmarks for quality education.

---

### **5. Creation of State Councils of Higher Education**

RUSA encourages the formation of **State Councils of Higher Education** to decentralize governance and improve planning at the state level.

---

### **6. New Funding Mechanism**

A revolutionary feature of RUSA is that central financial aid is routed through State Councils instead of directly through the UGC.

This ensures:

* Greater autonomy for states
* Better accountability
* Efficient utilization of funds

---

### **7. Funding Ratio**

For new expenditure under RUSA:

| Source             | Share |
| ------------------ | ----- |
| Central Government | 90%   |
| State Government   | 10%   |

This reduces the financial burden on states while ensuring strong central support.

---

### **8. Inclusion of Private Institutions**

Even private unaided colleges and universities can receive funds under RUSA, provided they meet quality standards and follow conditions prescribed by the scheme.

---

## **Conclusion**

Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) is a landmark reform programme for strengthening higher education in India. Along with SSA and RMSA, it creates a complete educational pathway from elementary to higher education. By improving funding, autonomy, infrastructure, and quality, RUSA plays a key role in building a strong and modern higher education system for national development.
